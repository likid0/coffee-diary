## Espresso system baseline established

Previous issue: persistent salty/mineral finish across all espressos.
Diagnosis: systemic under-extraction — ratio too low (1:1.9) and insufficient
pre-infusion time.

Resolution:
- Grind: 4 (significantly finer than previous range)
- Pre-infusion: 15s (hold two-cup button during pre-infusion phase)
- Yield: 44g out from 20g in (1:2.2 ratio)
- Time: ~32s
- Temp: 93°C standard, 94–95°C recommended for decafs and younger coffees

## Why saltiness = under-extraction

Extraction sequence: salty → sour → sweet → bitter.
Previous shots were stopping before the sweet fraction fully developed.
Salts and organic acids extract first — sweetness requires longer contact time
and/or finer grind to reach.

## Note on per-bean variation

Grind 4 is the new system baseline but individual beans will vary:
- Denser beans (high-altitude Ethiopians) may need coarser adjustment
- Decafs extract faster due to compromised cell structure
- Always use this baseline as starting point, not final reference
